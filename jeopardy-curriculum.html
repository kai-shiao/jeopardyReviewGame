CASE DESCRIPTION
FRENCH AND INDIAN WAR THIS WAR WOULD LATER SERVE AS AN INDIRECT CATALYST FOR THE AMERICAN REVOLUTION.
INTOLERABLE ACTS THESE ACTS BY THE BRITISH PARLIAMENT WERE ENACTED AS A PUNITIVE MEASURE IN RESPONSE TO THE BOSTON TEA PARTY. THE PROVISIONS INCLUDED CLOSING THE PORT OF BOSTON, ELIMINATING SELF-RULE FOR COLONIAL MASSACHUSETTS, APPOINTING A ROYAL GOVERNOR, & IMPLEMENTING ROYAL APPOINTMENTS FOR COLONIAL GOVERNMENT POSITIONS.
OLIVE BRANCH PETITION THIS PETITION WAS ADOPTED BY THE SECOND CONTINENTAL CONGRESS AS A LAST-DITCH ATTEMPT TO AVOID ALL-OUT ARMED CONFLICT WITH GREAT BRITAIN AND PRECEDED THE 'DECLARATION OF CAUSES AND NECESSITY OF TAKING UP ARMS'.
TAXATION WITHOUT REPRESENTATION THIS SLOGAN WAS USED TO SUM UP THE AMERICAN COLONISTS' OPPOSITION TO A SERIES OF VARIOUS REVENUE-RAISING ACTS ENACTED BY THE BRITISH PARLIAMENT AFTER THE END OF THE FRENCH AND INDIAN WAR.
COMMON SENSE THIS 1776 POLITICAL ESSAY AUTHORED BY THOMAS PAYNE AT THE OUTBREAK OF THE AMERICAN REVOLUTION BECAME VERY WIDELY READ THROUGHOUT THE AMERICAN COLONIES & HAD THE EFFECT OF RALLYING POPULAR OPINION IN FAVOUR OF INDEPENDENCE OF GREAT BRITAIN.

GILDED AGE
TERM DESCRIPTION
ROBBER BARONS THIS TERM REFERS TO THE CLASS OF OLIGARCHS WHO HAD EXTENSIVE INFLUENCE IN BOTH POLITICAL AND ECONOMIC SPHERES IN THE UNITED STATES DURING THIS ERA.
CARNEGIE THIS TYCOON MADE HIS FORTUNE IN THE STEEL INDUSTRY BY INTRODUCING & IMPLEMENTING BUSINESS PRACTICES SUCH AS VERTICAL INTEGRATION AND BELIEVED THAT CAPITALISM WAS ABOUT THE 'SURVIVAL OF THE FITTEST'.
PROGRESSIVE ERA THE PERCEIVED EXCESSES & ILLS THAT FLOURISHED DURING THE GILDED AGE BECAME THE TARGET OF ACTIVISTS & REFORMERS IN THIS ERA.
GOSPEL OF WEALTH THIS ARTICLE AUTHORED BY CARNEGIE EMPHASIZED THAT THE WEALTHY ALSO HAD A DUTY TO SOCIETY BY ADMINISTERING THEIR WEALTH TO ENSURE SURPLUS WEALTH WAS USED FOR PHILANTROPIC PURPOSES FOR THE GREATER BENEFIT OF SOCIETY.
VERTICAL INTEGRATION THIS BUSINESS PRACTICE INVOLVED OWNERSHIP AND CONTROL OF THE ENTIRE SUPPLY CHAIN IN ORDER TO MAXIMIZE BUSINESS EFFICIENCY AND THUS PROFITS. IN THIS CASE, A STEEL COMPANY WOULD ALSO OWN & CONTROL THE IRON ORE MINES AND THE RAILWAYS NEEDED TO TRANSPORT BOTH THE IRON ORE FROM MINES AND THE FINISHED STEEL TO ITS INTENDED MARKETS.
